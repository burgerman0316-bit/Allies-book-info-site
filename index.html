SUMMARY

The book begins with Douglas "Dee" Carpenter, a 16-year-old American soldier riding in a landing boat toward Omaha beach in France. He is nervous because he has never been in real combat before. His friend Sid Jacobstein is with him and tries to calm him down. Dee has a secret, his real name is Dietrich Zimmerman, he was actually born in Germany but escaped the Nazis when he was younger, and he joined the U.S. army to fight the Nazis to get back to what they did to his family. As the soldiers get closer to the beach, the Germans start shooting at them from bunkers. When the landing boat door opens, many soldiers are hit immediately. The beach becomes extremely chaotic, machine guns are firing, explosions are everywhere, and soldiers are trying to reach cover behind obstacles. Dee jumps into the water and struggles to survive while trying to move forward. The book switches perspective between perspectives in the book, one of them is an 11-year-old girl living in France, her name is Samira, her mother has been captured by the german soldiers, Samira wants to warn the french resistance that the allied invasion is starting, she risks her own life traveling through areas controlled by the Germans. The first part of the books shows how terrifying and confusing the invasion is. Soldiers are scared, wounded, or killed around Dee. Meanwhile, Samira tries to sneak past German guards. She must be brave and clever to avoid being caught.
